A few months ago I was in New York and played around with an iPhone for about half an hour. I really loved it and I immediately knew that I wanted to get one as soon as they would be available in the UK. At that time, a lot of reports circulated the web that described how to unlock the device and make it work on non AT&T networks. However, I wanted the phone for one particular reason: to surf the web when I'm on the run. Because Apple seems to be in a position where they can just ignore the European market, they decided to equip the phone with support for EDGE only and refrain from implementing support for much more up-to-date 3G standards and technologies that are available in Europe.

Apple's iPhone, however, seems to be cool enough, to make network providers adapt their technologies to one single device and so O2 started "upgrading" their network to EDGE a while ago. Since yesterday they are proudly offering the iPhone as part of an 18 months contract for a whopping £ 269.-. The contract costs £ 35.- per month and apparently the EDGE coverage doesn't go very far beyond major city hubs.

Doing the maths: I can get an iPhone for the equivalent of £ 190.-, so why on earth would I pay an extra £ 79.- just to lock myself into an 18 months contract?!

Ok, I know it's far from being perfect, but I still really like the iPhone. However, not so much as that I would volunteer to being ripped off and carry some extra cash to the feet of some companies that are swimming in money already.

Yet looking at the queues in front of most London phone shops yesterday evening, there still seems to be a good number of people that don't see it this way.
